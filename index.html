Top Bronx Middle Schools

(at getting into specialized high schools)

By Jacob Schermerhorn | Updated March 8, 2021

During a Mayoral Forum on Friday, student activist Toby Paperno surprised mayoral hopefuls with a pop quiz on poetry and math. None of them answered the questions correctly.

"To those of you who got some of these questions wrong, you are not as smart as your peers and will be denied entrance to the specialized high schools," Paperno said, "If you had a bad day and didn't get some of the questions right â€” I don't care and neither does the test."

The forum, which was held by student activist group Teens Take Charge, was focused on inequalities of the Specialized High School Assessment Test. Although the nine specialized high schools have recently relaxed their application process, activists, including TTC, claim the test is still biased toward students who are white and wealthy.

Using Specialized High School acceptance rate data released by the NYC Department of Education from 2015 to 2019, the most successful middle schools in the Bronx tended to be whiter and wealthier.

For example, at Riverdale Kingbridge Academy, one in four students are white and the poverty rates are the lowest in the bourough at 52%. The median population of white students in Bronx schools is 1.2%, the median poverty rate is 88%.
